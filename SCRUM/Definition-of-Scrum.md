## What is Scrum?

**Scrum** is a **lightweight framework** that helps people, teams, and organizations generate value through **adaptive solutions for complex problems**.

Scrum is:

- **Lightweight**
- **Simple to understand**
- **Difficult to master**

### What does this mean?

Scrum provides a **framework to implement the Agile mindset**.

When working on complex products where **not everything is known upfront**, the Scrum framework helps teams deal with **uncertainties and changing requirements**.

However, Scrum is **only a framework**:
- It provides **guidelines and rules**
- It **does not prescribe exactly how to do everything**

Figuring things out during the process is also part of being Agile:  
**learn → adapt → improve**

---

# The Foundation of Scrum

Scrum is based on **Empiricism**.

**Empiricism** means:
- Knowledge comes from **experience**
- Decisions should be based on **what is known**

In empiricism:
- Only the **past is accepted as certain**
- Decisions are based on **observation, experience, and experimentation**

Scrum uses this empirical process through **three pillars**:

1. Transparency  
2. Inspection  
3. Adaptation  

---

# The 3 Pillars of Empiricism

## 1. Transparency

Transparency ensures everyone understands the work and the process.

Requirements:

- A **common language** referring to the process must be shared by all participants
- Those performing the work and those accepting the product must share a common **Definition of Done**

---

## 2. Inspection

Scrum practitioners must **frequently inspect**:

- Scrum **artifacts**
- Progress toward the **Sprint Goal**

Purpose:
- Detect **undesirable variances**
- Identify problems early

---

## 3. Adaptation

If inspection shows the process is outside acceptable limits:

- The **process must be adjusted**
- The **product development must be adapted**

Goal: **continuous improvement**.

---

# Iterative and Incremental Development

## Iterative

**Iterative = repeating a process to improve it.**

- Gradual improvement
- Multiple cycles
- Each cycle is an **iteration**
- Focus on improving the **process**

---

## Incremental

**Incremental = building the product piece by piece.**

- Product divided into **components**
- Each component built separately
- Components delivered when complete

Focus: **the product grows over time**

---

> “We increment to adapt, we iterate to improve.”  
> — Jurgen Appelo

---

# Scrum is a Framework

Scrum is **not a process** and **not a technique**.

It is a **framework** where teams can use different processes and techniques.

The Scrum framework includes:

- **Roles**
- **Events**
- **Artifacts**
- **Rules**

---

# Scrum Process Overview

1. **Sprint Planning**  
   Decide what product parts to build and how.

2. **Daily Scrum**  
   Short daily meeting to track progress toward the **Sprint Goal**.

3. **Product Increment**  
   A working version of the product delivered at the end of the sprint.

4. **Sprint Review**  
   Stakeholders review the increment and give feedback.

5. **Sprint Retrospective**  
   The team reflects and improves future sprints.

---

# The 5 Scrum Values

According to the Scrum Guide:

1. **Commitment** – The team commits to achieving goals.  
2. **Focus** – Focus on the Sprint work.  
3. **Openness** – Be transparent about progress and challenges.  
4. **Respect** – Team members respect each other.  
5. **Courage** – Do the right thing and tackle difficult problems.
