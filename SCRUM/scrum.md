# Scrum Overview

**Scrum** is an **Agile framework** used to manage and deliver products through short iterations called **Sprints**.  

A **Sprint** usually lasts **1–4 weeks** and produces a **working increment of the product**.


## Scrum Roles

### 1. Product Owner
Responsibilities:
- Defines **what should be built**
- Manages the **Product Backlog** (list of features/tasks)
- Prioritizes work based on **business value**
- Decides **which features go into a sprint**

> Intuition: They **choose the features** to be worked on.

---

### 2. Scrum Master
Responsibilities:
- Removes **blockers/impediments**
- Ensures the team follows **Scrum practices**
- Helps the team stay **productive**
- Protects the team from outside distractions

> Intuition: They act as the **facilitator and problem-solver**.

---

### 3. Developers (Development Team)
Responsibilities:
- Implement the features
- Design, code, test, and integrate
- Deliver a **working product increment each sprint**
- Self-organizing — decide **how** the work gets done

---

## Scrum Events

### 1. Sprint
- A **time-boxed cycle** where the team builds features
- Duration: **1–4 weeks**
- Output: A **potentially shippable product increment**

---

### 2. Daily Scrum (Daily Stand-up)
- **15-minute daily meeting**
- Team members answer:
  1. What did I do yesterday?
  2. What will I do today?
  3. Are there any blockers?
- Goal: Keep the team **aligned and productive**

---

### 3. Sprint Review
- Happens **at the end of the sprint**
- Purpose:
  - Demonstrate the **working product**
  - Get **feedback from stakeholders**
  - Update the **product backlog**

---

### 4. Sprint Retrospective
- Happens **after the sprint**
- Purpose:
  - Reflect on **how the team worked**
  - Identify **improvements**
  - Improve the **process for the next sprint**

Example topics:
- What went well?
- What went wrong?
- What should we improve?

---

## Simple Scrum Flow

1. **Product Owner selects features**
2. Team plans the sprint
3. **Developers build features**
4. **Daily Scrum every day**
5. End of sprint → **Sprint Review**
6. Team improvement → **Sprint Retrospective**
7. Next **Sprint starts**

---

## Simple Definition

> Scrum = A framework where a team builds a product in **small iterations (sprints)** with **constant feedback and improvement**.
