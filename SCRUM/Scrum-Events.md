# Scrum Events

## Sprints Are Small Projects

Sprints function like **small projects** used to accomplish work in an iteration.

A **Sprint** is a short, fixed time period during which the team produces a **potentially shippable piece of software**.

Each Sprint includes:

- A **Sprint Goal**
- Analysis and design activities
- A flexible plan to create the **product increment**

An **Increment** is a piece of working product that adds to previously created increments.  
All increments together form the final **product**.

Key points:

- Each Sprint should have the **same length**
- A consistent rhythm helps create **predictability**
- A Sprint can only be **canceled by the Product Owner**
- Cancellation is rare and usually happens when the **Sprint Goal becomes obsolete**

---

# The Sprint

A **Sprint** is a **time-boxed event of one month or less** that contains all other Scrum events and activities.

Sprints run **continuously without gaps**.

The Sprint is the **core of Scrum**.

During a Sprint, the team creates a **“Done” Increment**.

**Done** means the product is:

- Usable
- Potentially releasable to users

A Sprint includes:

- Sprint Planning
- Daily Scrums
- Development work
- Sprint Review
- Sprint Retrospective

---

# Sprint Planning

**Sprint Planning** is the **first event of every Sprint**.

The **entire Scrum Team** participates.

Sprint Planning answers **three main questions**:

## 1. Why is the Sprint valuable?

The Scrum Team defines the **Sprint Goal**, which communicates the value of the Sprint to stakeholders.

## 2. What can be delivered?

Inputs for this discussion:

- The **ordered Product Backlog**
- The latest **product Increment**

Developers select **top-priority Product Backlog Items (PBIs)** based on previous performance.

## 3. How will the work be done?

Developers:

- Discuss the work
- Break it down
- Design solutions
- Identify tasks

This creates the **Sprint Backlog**, which contains ordered tasks and requirements.

### In Short

**Time-box**

- Up to **8 hours for a one-month Sprint**
- Shorter for shorter Sprints

**Participants**

- Entire **Scrum Team**

**Objective**

Create a plan for the Sprint:

- Define the **Sprint Goal**
- Decide **what Increment will be built**
- Determine **how the work will be completed**

---

# Daily Scrum

The **Daily Scrum** is a short daily meeting for **Developers**.

Purpose:

- Synchronize work
- Plan the next **24 hours**

Developers inspect progress toward the **Sprint Goal** and adjust plans if needed.

Common discussion questions:

- What did I do **yesterday** to help meet the Sprint Goal?
- What will I do **today** to help meet the Sprint Goal?
- Do I see any **impediments** blocking progress?

Benefits:

- Improves communication
- Reduces the need for additional meetings
- Identifies obstacles quickly
- Encourages fast decision-making
- Keeps the team aligned

### In Short

**Time-box**

- **15 minutes**

**Participants**

- Developers (required)
- Scrum Master and Product Owner may attend but should not disrupt

**Objectives**

- Inspect progress toward the **Sprint Goal**
- Keep the team **aligned and informed**

---

# Sprint Review

The **Sprint Review** occurs **at the end of the Sprint**.

Purpose:

- Inspect the **product Increment**
- Adapt the **Product Backlog**

During the review:

- Developers explain how the Sprint went
- Developers demonstrate the completed work
- Developers answer questions about the Increment
- The Product Owner discusses the Product Backlog
- Stakeholders provide feedback
- The group collaborates on what to do next

Outcome:

- A **revised Product Backlog** with updated priorities for future Sprints.

### In Short

**Time-box**

- **4 hours for a one-month Sprint**
- Shorter for shorter Sprints

**Participants**

- Scrum Team
- Stakeholders

**Objectives**

- Collaborate with stakeholders
- Inspect the **potentially shippable Increment**

---

# Sprint Retrospective

The **Sprint Retrospective** happens:

- After the **Sprint Review**
- Before the **next Sprint Planning**

Purpose:

- Inspect how the Sprint went
- Improve the way the team works

The Scrum Team evaluates:

- People
- Relationships
- Processes
- Tools

The team then:

- Identifies what went well
- Identifies improvements
- Creates an **action plan for the next Sprint**

The **Definition of Done** may also be reviewed and improved if needed.

### In Short

**Time-box**

- **3 hours for a one-month Sprint**
- Shorter for shorter Sprints

**Participants**

- Scrum Team

**Objectives**

- Inspect and improve teamwork
- Decide on **improvements for the next Sprint**

---

# Product Backlog Refinement

**Product Backlog Refinement** is the activity of improving Product Backlog Items.

This includes:

- Adding **details**
- Creating **estimates**
- Adjusting the **order of items**

The goal is to prepare PBIs so they can be **completed within a Sprint**.

Refinement is an **ongoing collaboration** between:

- Product Owner
- Developers

Because it has **no official time-box**, it is **not considered a formal Scrum event**.

### In Short

**Time-box**

- No official time-box
- Usually **less than 10% of Developer capacity**

**Participants**

- Scrum Team

**Objective**

- Prepare Product Backlog Items for upcoming Sprints
- Add **detail, estimates, and ordering**
