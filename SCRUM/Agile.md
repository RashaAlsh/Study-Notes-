## Agile Definition
**Agile** is a **mindset for managing complex projects**, especially software development.

Key ideas:
- Deals with **uncertainty and changing requirements**
- Focuses on **customer satisfaction**
- Delivers work **incrementally**
- Uses **short development cycles called iterations**

---

# Iterations
**Iterations** = short development cycles where the product is improved step-by-step.

Characteristics:
- Small pieces of functionality delivered
- Frequent feedback
- Continuous improvement

---

# Waterfall vs Agile

| Waterfall | Agile |
|-----------|-------|
| Traditional project method | Adaptive project method |
| Plan everything at the start | Plans evolve during project |
| Fixed phases | Iterative cycles |
| Hard to change requirements | Easy to adapt to change |
| Best when requirements are clear | Best when requirements are uncertain |

### When Waterfall Works Best
- Requirements **known upfront**
- **Clear final goal**
- **Predictable phases**

### When Agile Works Best
- Requirements **change frequently**
- **Solution not yet clear**
- **Learning during development**

---

# Why Agile?

Modern projects involve:
- Rapid changes
- Evolving requirements
- Technological uncertainty

Agile helps by using:
- **Short iterations**
- **Fast feedback loops**
- **Continuous adaptation**

---

# Agile Manifesto

Created in **2001 by 17 software developers**.

Purpose:
- Replace **heavy documentation-based development**
- Promote **people, collaboration, and flexibility**

The manifesto defines **4 core values**.

---

# Agile Manifesto Values

Agile values:

1. **Individuals and interactions**  
   over processes and tools

2. **Working software**  
   over comprehensive documentation

3. **Customer collaboration**  
   over contract negotiation

4. **Responding to change**  
   over following a plan

Important idea:
> Items on the right still have value, but **items on the left are valued more.**
