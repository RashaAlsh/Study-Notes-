The *Scrum Team* consists of:

- *1 Product Owner*
- *1 Scrum Master*
- *Developers*

Scrum Teams are *self-managing* and *cross-functional*.

- *Self-managing*: the team decides how to accomplish the work instead of being directed by people outside the team.
- *Cross-functional*: the team has all the skills needed to complete the work.

The Scrum team model is based on:

- Flexibility
- Creativity
- Productivity

Scrum Teams deliver products *iteratively* and *incrementally* by working closely together as *one team*.

---

# Product Owner

The *Product Owner* is responsible for *maximizing the value of the product*.

They mainly do this by *managing and expressing business and functional expectations* for the product to the Development Team.

### Responsibilities

The Product Owner:

- Acts as the *business representative*
- Is accountable for the *Product Vision*
- Manages the *Product Backlog*
- *Orders and prioritizes Product Backlog Items (PBIs)*
- Tracks *progress, value, ROI (Return on Investment), and TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)*
- Inspects *working software*

Important notes:

- The Product Owner has *authority over requirements*
- The Product Owner does *not control estimates or team members*
- There is *only one Product Owner per Scrum Team*
- A Product Owner may work with *multiple Scrum Teams*

---

# Scrum Master

The *Scrum Master* ensures that *Scrum is understood and properly applied*.

### Responsibilities

The Scrum Master acts as:

- *Facilitator* of Scrum events
- *Coach and guide* in Agile and empirical thinking
- *Teacher* of Scrum theory, practices, and rules
- *Remover of impediments* blocking the team's progress
- *Servant-leader* for the Product Owner, Developers, and the organization
- *Change agent* helping the organization adopt Scrum

Important notes:

- There is *only one Scrum Master per Scrum Team*
- A Scrum Master may support *multiple Scrum Teams*

---

# Developers

*Developers* are the professionals who create the *product Increment* during a Sprint.

They are responsible for delivering a *usable, potentially releasable product increment* at the end of each Sprint.

Developers are *cross-functional*, meaning the team has all the skills needed to complete the work without relying on people outside the team.

### Responsibilities

Developers have:

- *Collective accountability* to turn *Product Backlog Items (PBIs)* into working increments
- Responsibility to follow *engineering standards* and the *Definition of Done*
- *Collective ownership of the code*
- Authority over:
  - *PBI estimates*
  - *Sprint Backlog*
  - *Team collaboration and work execution*

---

# Stakeholders

*Stakeholders* are individuals or groups *outside the Scrum Team* who have an interest in the product.

They may provide:

- Feedback
- Requirements
- Domain knowledge

Stakeholders are involved in the *Sprint Review*.

During the *Sprint Review*:

- The Scrum Team presents the work completed in the Sprint
- Stakeholders provide feedback
- The team discusses possible next steps

Important rule:

Developers *should not directly act on stakeholder requests*.

Instead:

Stakeholders must communicate their requests through the *Product Owner, who acts as the **single point of contact for product requirements*.

---

# Summary

The *Scrum Team* consists of three roles:

1. *Scrum Master*
2. *Product Owner*
3. *Developers*

Key ideas:

- Scrum Teams are *self-managing*
- Teams are *cross-functional*
- Work is delivered *iteratively and incrementally*
- Stakeholders collaborate mainly during the *Sprint Review*
