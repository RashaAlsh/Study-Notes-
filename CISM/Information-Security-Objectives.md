# CISM – Information Security Objectives

## Definition
Information security objectives define what an organization aims to achieve to protect its information assets and support business goals.

---

## Core Objectives (CIA Triad)

### 1. Confidentiality
- Ensure information is accessible **only to authorized users**
- Prevent unauthorized disclosure

### 2. Integrity
- Ensure information is **accurate, complete, and reliable**
- Prevent unauthorized modification

### 3. Availability
- Ensure information and systems are **accessible when needed**
- Minimize downtime and disruptions

---

## Supporting Objectives

### Authentication
- Verify the identity of users and systems

### Authorization
- Ensure users have **appropriate access rights**

### Accountability
- Track user actions (logging, monitoring, auditing)

### Non-Repudiation
- Prevent denial of actions (e.g., digital signatures)

---

## Business Alignment

- Must align with **organizational goals**
- Support **risk management**
- Protect **critical assets**
- Enable **secure operations**

---

## Key Concepts

- Security objectives are **business-driven**, not just technical
- Based on **risk appetite defined by the Board**
- Implemented through **policies, controls, and procedures**

---

## Exam Tips

- CIA Triad is always the **foundation**
- Think: **“Protect → Detect → Respond”**
- Security exists to **support the business**, not block it

---
