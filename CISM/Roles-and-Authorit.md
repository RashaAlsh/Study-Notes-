# CISM – Roles and Line of Authority

## Line of Authority
The line of authority defines the chain of command and decision-making power within an organization.

**Hierarchy:**
Board of Directors → CEO → CIO → CISO → Security Manager → Staff

- Authority flows **downward**
- Accountability remains **at the top**

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Board of Directors
- Defines risk appetite
- Provides governance and oversight
- Approves security strategy

### CEO (Chief Executive Officer)
- Ultimately accountable for information security
- Aligns security with business objectives
- Approves major decisions and risk acceptance

### CIO (Chief Information Officer)
- Manages IT strategy and operations
- Ensures IT supports business and security needs

### CISO (Chief Information Security Officer)
- Develops information security strategy
- Establishes policies, standards, and controls
- Reports risk posture to senior management

### Security Manager
- Implements security strategy
- Manages daily security operations
- Oversees incident response and controls

### Staff (Analysts, Admins, Engineers)
- Executes security controls
- Monitors systems and responds to threats

---

## Key Concepts

- **Authority** = Decision-making power  
- **Responsibility** = تنفيذ العمل (execution of tasks)  
- **Accountability** = Ownership of outcomes (CEO / Board)

---

## Exam Tips

- CEO is **accountable**, not the CISO  
- Board defines **risk appetite**  
- CISO focuses on **strategy**, not execution  
- Security Manager handles **implementation**  
- Staff performs **operations**
