# CMMI – Capability Maturity Model Integration

## Definition
CMMI is a framework used to assess and improve the **maturity of organizational processes**, including information security processes.

---

## Purpose
- Improve process efficiency and effectiveness
- Standardize organizational processes
- Enable continuous improvement
- Support risk reduction through mature practices

---

## Maturity Levels

### Level 1 – Initial
- Processes are **ad hoc and chaotic**
- No formal structure
- Success depends on individuals

### Level 2 – Managed
- Basic project/process management exists
- Processes are **repeatable**
- Reactive but controlled

### Level 3 – Defined
- Processes are **standardized and documented**
- Organization-wide consistency

### Level 4 – Quantitatively Managed
- Processes are **measured and controlled**
- Uses metrics and KPIs

### Level 5 – Optimizing
- Focus on **continuous improvement**
- Uses feedback and innovation

---

## Key Concepts

- Higher maturity = **more predictable and secure processes**
- Focus shifts from **reactive → proactive → optimized**
- Helps improve **governance and risk management**

---

## CISM Exam Focus

- CMMI measures **process maturity**, not security tools
- Used to evaluate **how well security processes are managed**
- Higher maturity = better **risk control and governance**
