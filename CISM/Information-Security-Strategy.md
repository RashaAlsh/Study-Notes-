## Definition
Information security strategy is a **high-level plan** that outlines how an organization will protect its information assets while supporting business objectives.

---

## Purpose
- Align security with **business goals**
- Manage and reduce **risk**
- Protect **critical information assets**
- Ensure **compliance** with laws and regulations

---

## Key Components

### Business Alignment
- Security must support **organizational objectives**
- Driven by **business needs**, not technology

### Risk Management
- Identify, assess, and treat risks
- Based on **risk appetite defined by the Board**

### Governance
- Defines **roles, responsibilities, and authority**
- Ensures accountability and oversight

### Policies and Standards
- High-level policies guide security direction
- Standards and procedures support implementation

### Resource Management
- Allocate **budget, people, and tools**
- Ensure efficient use of resources

### Metrics and Monitoring
- Measure effectiveness (KPIs, KRIs)
- Continuous improvement

---

## Characteristics of a Good Strategy

- **Aligned with business goals**
- **Risk-based**
- **Approved by senior management**
- **Flexible and adaptable**
- **Measurable**

---

## Roles Involved

- **Board of Directors** → Approves and sets direction  
- **CEO** → Accountable  
- **CISO** → Develops and maintains strategy  
- **Security Manager** → Implements strategy  

---

## Key Concepts

- Strategy = **“WHAT and WHY”**  
- Implementation = **“HOW”**

---

## Exam Tips

- Business alignment is the **MOST important factor**
- Strategy must be **risk-based**
- CISO creates strategy, not implements it
- Always think **top-down approach**

---

## Quick Memory Trick

> Align → Assess Risk → Govern → Implement → Measure
