# CISM Testing: Tabletop Exercise (TTX)

## 1. Definition
A discussion-based exercise where personnel with high-level roles meet in a classroom setting to discuss their actions during a simulated emergency.

## 2. Key Participants
* **Facilitator:** Leads the discussion and provides "Injects."
* **Players:** The incident response team (CISO, IT, Legal, HR, Communications).
* **Evaluator/Scribe:** Records the decisions and identifies gaps.

## 3. Success Metrics (KPIs)
* **Time to Escalate:** How long did it take to notify senior management?
* **Communication Accuracy:** Was the information shared between teams correct?
* **Plan Familiarity:** Did participants know where the BCP documents were stored?
* **Gap Identification:** Number of "unknowns" discovered during the session.

## 4. Documentation
The output of a TTX is the **After-Action Report (AAR)**, which includes:
1. Scenario overview.
2. Strengths identified.
3. Areas for improvement (The "Gaps").
4. A formal **Remediation Plan**.
