# Cheat Sheet: Governance vs. Management in Information Security

It is a common misconception that Governance and Management are the same. In reality, they represent two distinct layers of organizational control. A helpful mnemonic: **Governance** determines the *direction*, while **Management** handles the *execution*.

---

## Governance (The "Heads")
**Ownership:** Board of Directors, Executive Leadership, and Steering Committees.

Governance ensures that stakeholder needs, conditions, and options are evaluated to determine balanced, agreed-on enterprise objectives to be achieved.

* **Focus:** Strategic alignment, oversight, and accountability.
* **Key Activities (EDM Model):**
    * **Evaluate:** Assessing the business environment and stakeholder requirements.
    * **Direct:** Setting the strategy, policies, and "Risk Appetite."
    * **Monitor:** Overseeing performance and compliance against agreed-upon direction.
* **Primary Question:** "Are we doing the right things for the business?"



---

## Management (The "Hands")
**Ownership:** CISO, Security Managers, and Technical Leads.

Management plans, builds, runs, and monitors activities in alignment with the direction set by the governance body to achieve the enterprise objectives.

* **Focus:** Operations, tactical execution, and technical implementation.
* **Key Activities (PBRM Model):**
    * **Plan:** Designing the security architecture.
    * **Build:** Implementing technical controls (Firewalls, MFA, Encryption).
    * **Run:** Managing day-to-day security operations and patching.
    * **Monitor:** Reviewing system logs and technical performance.
* **Primary Question:** "Are we doing things the right way (and efficiently)?"

---

## ⚖️ Comparison Table

| Feature | Governance | Management |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Objective** | Defining the "What" and "Why." | Defining the "How" and "When." |
| **Perspective** | Holistic and Enterprise-wide. | Task and Process oriented. |
| **Main Outcome** | Policies and Risk Appetite. | Procedures and Control implementation. |
| **Constraint** | Budget and Legal requirements. | Policy and Resource limitations. |
| **Frequency** | Periodic (Quarterly/Annually). | Continuous (Daily/Real-time). |

---

##  The Feedback Loop
Governance and Management must work in tandem to be effective:

1.  **Top-Down:** Governance provides the **Policies** and **Strategy** that Management must follow.
2.  **Bottom-Up:** Management provides **Metrics** and **Performance Data** so Governance can make informed decisions.

> **Pro Tip:** If a security team is choosing which firewall to buy without a policy stating what needs protection, they are "Managing" without "Governance"—this leads to wasted budget and security gaps.
