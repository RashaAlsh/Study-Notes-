# ISO/IEC 27000 Glossary of Key Terms

This is a reference for key terms and definitions from the ISO/IEC 27000 standard, used across the Information Security Management System (ISMS) family.

---

## A–Z Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Access Control** | Measure that limits access to information or resources based on authorization. |
| **Asset** | Anything that has value to the organization—premises, information, software, hardware, paper records, services, people, skills, experience, or intangibles like reputation and image. |
| **Audit** | Systematic, independent, and documented process for obtaining audit evidence and evaluating it objectively to determine the extent to which the audit criteria are fulfilled. |
| **Authentication** | Provision of assurance that a claimed characteristic of an entity is correct. |
| **Authenticity** | Property that an entity is what it claims to be. |
| **Attack** | Attempt to destroy, expose, alter, disable, steal, or gain unauthorized access to, or make unauthorized use of, an asset. |
| **Availability** | Property of being accessible and usable upon demand by an authorized entity. |
| **Competence** | Ability to apply knowledge and skills to achieve intended results. |
| **Confidentiality** | Property that information is not made available or disclosed to unauthorized individuals, entities, or processes. |
| **Consequence** | Outcome of an event affecting objectives; in risk context, usually negative (loss). |
| **Control** | Measure (policy, process, practice, or procedure) applied to manage risk. |
| **Control Objective** | Statement of intent specifying what is to be achieved by implementing selected controls. |
| **Correction** | Action to eliminate a detected nonconformity. |
| **Conformity** | Fulfillment of a requirement. |
| **Continual Improvement** | Ongoing effort to enhance the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the ISMS. |
| **Integrity** | Property of safeguarding the accuracy and completeness of assets; preventing unauthorized modification. |
| **Information Security** | Preservation of confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information. |
| **Information Security Event** | Occurrence or change of circumstances that affects the security of information. |
| **Information Security Incident** | Information security event or series of events that compromise or could compromise confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information. |
| **Information Security Management System (ISMS)** | Part of an organization’s management system based on a risk management approach to establish, implement, operate, monitor, review, maintain, and improve information security. |
| **Interested Party / Stakeholder** | Person or organization that can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself affected by information security decisions. |
| **Likelihood** | Chance that something might happen (used in risk analysis). |
| **Management System** | System to establish policy and objectives and to achieve those objectives; in ISMS, manages information security risks. |
| **Measure / Measurement** | Process of determining a value or magnitude to monitor performance, often used to track control effectiveness. |
| **Nonconformity** | Non-fulfillment of a requirement. |
| **Non-repudiation** | Assurance that the sender cannot deny sending information and the recipient cannot deny receiving it. |
| **Objective** | Something aimed at or intended by an organization (e.g., an information security objective). |
| **Organization** | Person or group of people with functions, responsibilities, authorities, and relationships. |
| **Policy** | Statement of intent and direction issued by top management. |
| **Process** | Set of interrelated or interacting activities that transforms inputs into outputs. |
| **Requirement** | Need or expectation that is stated, generally implied, or obligatory. |
| **Risk** | Effect of uncertainty on objectives; often expressed in terms of likelihood and consequence. |
| **Risk Assessment** | Overall process of risk identification, analysis, and evaluation. |
| **Risk Treatment** | Process to select and implement measures to modify risk. |
| **Threat** | Potential cause of an unwanted incident, which may result in harm to a system or organization. |
| **Vulnerability** | Weakness of an asset or control that can be exploited by a threat. |

---

## Notes
- The definitions help ensure consistent interpretation by organizations, auditors, and certification bodies.
