# Information Security Risk Management

##  Overview

Risk in information security is the **potential for loss or damage** when a **threat exploits a vulnerability**, impacting an organization.

> **Risk = Likelihood × Impact**

---

## 1.  Key Definitions

### Risk

The combination of:

* A **threat**
* Exploiting a **vulnerability**
* Resulting in **business impact**

---

### Threat

An event or actor that can cause harm.

**Examples:**

* Human: hackers, insiders
* Environmental: floods, fires
* Technical: malware, system failures

---

### Vulnerability

A weakness that can be exploited.

**Examples:**

* Unpatched software
* Weak passwords
* Misconfigured systems

---

### Exposure

The state of being vulnerable to damage or loss.

> Example: Weak password policies expose systems to unauthorized access.

---

### Security Measures (Controls)

Safeguards to reduce risk:

* **Preventive** → Stop incidents

  * Firewalls, MFA

* **Detective** → Identify incidents

  * Logging, intrusion detection

* **Corrective** → Recover from incidents

  * Backups, incident response

---

## 2.  Risk Management Process

### 1. Risk Identification

* Identify assets, threats, and vulnerabilities
* Example: Database vulnerable to SQL injection

---

### 2. Risk Analysis

Assess:

* **Likelihood** (probability)
* **Impact** (damage)

**Methods:**

* Qualitative → High / Medium / Low
* Quantitative → Financial estimates

---

### 3. Risk Evaluation

* Compare risks against acceptable levels
* Decide if risk is tolerable

---

### 4. Risk Treatment

* Select and apply appropriate controls

>  This is a continuous cycle, not a one-time process.

---

## 3.  Risk Treatment Strategies

### Mitigation (Reduce Risk)

* Apply controls to lower likelihood or impact
* Example: Patch systems, enforce MFA

---

### Avoidance (Eliminate Risk)

* Stop the risky activity
* Example: Do not store sensitive data

---

### Transfer (Share Risk)

* Shift risk to another party
* Example: Insurance, outsourcing

---

### Acceptance (Retain Risk)

* Accept risk without further action
* Used when mitigation cost > potential loss

---

## 4. 💡 Key Insight

> **No vulnerability = no risk**
> **No threat = no risk**

Risk exists only when both are present.

---

## 5.  Example Scenario

| Component     | Example             |
| ------------- | ------------------- |
| Asset         | Web Application     |
| Vulnerability | Unpatched framework |
| Threat        | External attacker   |
| Risk          | Data breach         |
| Control       | Patch management    |

---

##  Summary

Effective risk management:

* Identifies risks
* Analyzes impact
* Applies appropriate controls
* Continuously monitors and improves
