# Physical Controls (ISO/IEC 27002:2022)

Physical controls protect an organization’s **tangible assets**—including buildings, equipment, and people—from threats such as unauthorized access, theft, fire, and natural disasters.

These controls are grouped into:
- **Physical Security Perimeters**
- **Equipment Security**

---

## 1. Physical Security Perimeters (Barriers)

These controls create layered protection, following the principle of **Defense in Depth**.

- **Physical Security Boundary**
  - Walls, fences, gates, reception desks
  - Clearly separates public and secure areas

- **Physical Entry Controls**
  - Restrict access to authorized personnel only
  - Examples:
    - ID badges
    - Biometric scanners
    - Visitor logbooks

- **Securing Offices, Rooms, and Facilities**
  - Sensitive areas (e.g., server rooms) require extra protection
  - Ideally located away from windows and public access points

- **Physical Security Monitoring**
  - Real-time monitoring using:
    - CCTV
    - Motion sensors
    - Alarm systems

---

## 2. Equipment Security

Protect individual devices and infrastructure within secure environments.

- **Equipment Siting and Protection**
  - Place hardware to minimize risks:
    - Avoid water sources (pipes, leaks)
    - Prevent unauthorized viewing (screen positioning)

- **Supporting Utilities**
  - Ensure operational continuity:
    - UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)
    - Generators
    - HVAC (temperature and humidity control)

- **Cabling Security**
  - Protect cables from:
    - Interception
    - Interference
    - Physical damage

- **Storage Media**
  - Secure handling of removable media:
    - USB drives
    - External hard drives
  - Proper destruction when no longer needed

---

## Key Concepts for the Exam

| Control                     | Description |
|----------------------------|-------------|
| **Clear Desk & Clear Screen** | Remove sensitive materials and lock screens when unattended |
| **Delivery & Loading Areas**  | Isolate these areas to prevent unauthorized access to secure zones |
| **Maintenance**               | Regular servicing of security systems (cameras, locks, fire equipment) |

---

## Critical Note

Physical security is not only about **preventing unauthorized access**, but also about **ensuring safety**.

- Fire suppression systems  
- Emergency exits  
- Evacuation procedures  

These controls protect the organization’s most important asset: **its people**.

---

## Summary

Physical controls:
- Provide layered defense against physical threats  
- Protect both infrastructure and personnel  
- Support overall organizational resilience  

They are a crucial complement to **people**, **organizational**, and **technological** controls.
