# People Controls (ISO/IEC 27002:2022)

People controls address the **human factor** in information security. Since many breaches are caused by human error or insider threats, these controls ensure that individuals understand their responsibilities and are suitable for their roles.

The lifecycle is divided into three stages:
- **Before Employment**
- **During Employment**
- **After Employment**

---

## 1. Before Employment (Screening)

The goal is to ensure that candidates are trustworthy and qualified.

- **Screening**
  - Background checks (identity, qualifications, criminal records)
  - Level of screening depends on role sensitivity

- **Terms and Conditions**
  - Employment contracts must define:
    - Security responsibilities
    - Consequences of non-compliance

---

## 2. During Employment (Active Phase)

Employees must be actively managed and continuously educated.

- **Management Responsibilities**
  - Ensure staff follow security policies and procedures

- **Information Security Awareness, Education, and Training**
  - One of the most critical controls
  - Regular training on:
    - Threats (e.g., phishing)
    - Company policies and procedures

- **Disciplinary Process**
  - Formal procedures for handling violations
  - Ensures accountability and enforcement

---

## 3. After Employment (Termination or Change)

Security risks often increase when employees leave or change roles.

- **Termination Responsibilities**
  - Formal offboarding process required

- **Return of Assets**
  - Collect all company property:
    - Laptops
    - Keys
    - ID cards

- **Revocation of Access Rights**
  - Immediately disable access to:
    - Systems
    - Email accounts
    - Databases

---

## Key Concepts

| Control                     | Description |
|----------------------------|-------------|
| **Confidentiality Agreements** | NDAs that protect sensitive information even after employment ends |
| **Remote Working**             | Policies ensuring home environments do not compromise security |
| **Onboarding / Offboarding**   | Processes for granting and removing access and assets |

---

## Why It Matters

"People are the weakest link" in information security.

Even the strongest technical controls (e.g., firewalls) can fail if:
- An employee is tricked into revealing credentials
- Security procedures are ignored

---

## Summary

People controls ensure:
- Trustworthy hiring practices  
- Continuous security awareness  
- Proper handling of employee departures  

They are essential for reducing human-related security risks and supporting all other control types.
