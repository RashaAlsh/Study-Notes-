# CIA Triad in Information Security

The **CIA Triangle** (also known as the **CIA Triad**) represents the three core principles of an information security program:

- **Confidentiality**
- **Integrity**
- **Availability**

For decades, this model has been fundamental in embedding security within organizations.

---

## 1. Confidentiality (Exclusivity)

Confidentiality ensures that information is not disclosed to unauthorized individuals, entities, or processes.

### Purpose
Prevent both intentional and unintentional disclosure of information.

### Protection Mechanisms
- Data encryption (at rest and in transit)
- Strict access control
- Data classification
- Network traffic padding
- Personnel training

---

## 2. Integrity

Integrity ensures that information remains accurate, complete, and consistent with its intended state.

### Purpose
Protect systems and data from unauthorized modification or accidental changes.

### Measures

#### Authorization
- Ensures all changes are verified and approved  
- Example: One person enters data, another verifies it  

#### Logging
- Records user actions  
- Helps track who made changes  

#### Segregation of Duties
- Prevents a single individual from performing critical actions alone  

#### Technical Controls
- Access controls  
- Intrusion detection systems (IDS)  
- Hashing for data verification  

---

## 3. Availability

Availability ensures that authorized users have reliable and timely access to systems and data.

### Characteristics
- **Timeliness** – Data is available when needed  
- **Continuity** – Operations continue during failures  
- **Robustness** – Systems handle required workloads  

### Threats
- Disk failures  
- Power outages  
- Environmental issues (heat, humidity)  
- Denial-of-Service (DoS) attacks  

### Protection Measures
- Backup systems  
- Redundant infrastructure  
- Network monitoring (firewalls, IDS)  

---

## Evolution of the CIA Triad

While the CIA Triad remains essential, modern standards have expanded its scope.

### Additional Attributes (ISO/IEC 27002:2022)
- Authenticity  
- Accountability  
- Non-repudiation  
- Reliability  

### Cybersecurity Framework Functions
- Identify  
- Protect  
- Detect  
- Respond  
- Recover  

---

## Summary

The CIA Triad continues to serve as the foundation of information security, but modern frameworks expand it to address evolving threats and organizational needs.
